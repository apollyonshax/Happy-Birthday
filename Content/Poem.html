My North Star

One starlit night, the moon’s collide
There’s a fire in the sky, like a blazing tide
Can’t see but I must, it’s a matter of pride
Thought I’d witness the apocalypse, so why do I see my bride?

I look up towards the heavens at that blinding light
Try as I may, I could never even dream up such a sight
The creation of God, an unattainable height
Still, how can a mere mortal shine that bright?

I realize it’s not her but me that God blessed
Made me able to love unconditionally, no feeling repressed
It isn’t blasphemy, just feelings that I confess
She may not be divinity, but she is celeste

As I gaze upon that star, I do wonder if she’ll be mine
At times I’m sure, at times I need a sign
I guess it doesn’t matter if our fate is tied with ropes or twine
If at the end she’s happy, I’m sure I’ll be fine

But let me dream just one more night of her warm embrace
Let me cherish these feelings, these memories, her face
I hope we end up together as lovers but if that is not the case
Then just know I’ll love you forever, and Happy Birthday, my grace
